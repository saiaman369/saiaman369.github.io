The Differentiator

I Own the Full Loop

Most battery engineers own one piece of the development chain. They characterize powders but never build cells, or they assemble and test but cannot trace a failure back to the material. I do both. The connection between them is where the value lives.

At FONASE, I was the sole technical contributor building a novel graphene-based anode platform from scratch. No supervisor. No lab budget. No inherited process. I selected materials, formulated electrode chemistries, fabricated electrodes, ran characterization, built coin cells, and interpreted the electrochemical data to drive the next design iteration.

That loop — from material decision to cell performance to next design choice — produced a granted U.S. Patent and performance results that hold up to technical scrutiny. It is also exactly what a battery manufacturer needs on an electrode or anode development team.

Featured Work

Graphene Anode Platform

FONASE Inc. · Chicago, IL · Battery Materials Startup
Graphene-Based Li-ion Anode Development
Nov 2021 – Present R&D Lead & Co-Founder Sole Technical Contributor
The Challenge

Build a novel graphene-based anode platform from zero — no inherited process, no lab infrastructure, no research team. Develop electrode chemistries that demonstrably outperform conventional Li-ion coin cells, validate them electrochemically, and produce protectable IP under real startup resource constraints.

What I Built
  • Developed two proprietary graphene electrode architectures — a co-doped porous graphene anode and a reduced graphene electrode — via solution-based fabrication, eliminating the need for industrial deposition equipment
  • Designed and iterated electrode chemistries across active material selection, binder systems, conductive additive optimization, and electrolyte formulation including ionic liquid systems with carbonate additives
  • Conducted full materials characterization: SEM, XRD, Raman, XPS, BET to assess morphology, graphitic structure, doping levels, and surface area
  • Assembled and tested 2032 coin cells across 32+ charge/discharge cycles, analyzing capacity retention, coulombic efficiency, impedance evolution, and SEI behavior
  • Coordinated cell testing and validation with external partners at Illinois Institute of Technology and Northwestern University (NUfab cleanroom)

Cell Architecture

2032 Coin Cell Architecture

The cell uses a co-doped porous graphene anode paired with an optimized ionic liquid electrolyte system. The architecture was designed from scratch using a solution-based fabrication process, deliberately avoiding industrial deposition equipment to maintain scalability.

Separator selection (Celgard), electrolyte formulation, and current collector pairing were each iterated based on electrochemical data. Every design decision traces back to a specific performance observation from galvanostatic cycling, EIS, or dQ/dV analysis.
