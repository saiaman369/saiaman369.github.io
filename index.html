The Differentiator

I Own the Full Loop

Most battery engineers own one piece of the development chain. They characterize powders but never build cells, or they assemble and test but can't trace a failure back to the material. I do both — and the connection between them is where the value lives.

At FONASE, I was the sole technical contributor building a novel graphene-based anode platform from the ground up. No supervisor. No lab budget. No inherited process. I selected materials, formulated electrode chemistries, fabricated electrodes, ran characterization, built coin cells, and interpreted the electrochemical data to drive the next iteration.

That loop — from material decision to cell performance to next design choice — is what produced a granted U.S. Patent and performance results that hold up to technical scrutiny. It's also exactly what a battery manufacturer needs on a materials or electrode development team.

Graphene Anode Platform

⏱ Nov 2021 – Present 🏛 R&D Lead & Co-Founder 🔬 Sole Technical Contributor
The Challenge

Build a novel graphene-based anode platform from zero — no inherited process, no lab infrastructure, no research team. Develop electrode chemistries that demonstrably outperform conventional Li-ion coin cells, validate them electrochemically, and produce protectable IP under real startup resource constraints.

What I Built
  • Developed two proprietary graphene electrode architectures (NSPG and TRG) — nitrogen and sulfur co-doped porous graphene systems — via solution-based fabrication, eliminating the need for industrial deposition equipment
  • Designed and iterated electrode chemistries: active material selection, binder systems, conductive additive optimization, and electrolyte formulation including ionic liquid systems with carbonate additives
  • Conducted full materials characterization suite: SEM, XRD, Raman, XPS, BET to assess morphology, graphitic structure, doping levels, and surface area
  • Assembled and tested 2032 coin cells; analyzed 32+ charge/discharge cycles for capacity retention, coulombic efficiency, impedance evolution, and SEI behavior
  • Coordinated cell testing and validation with external partners at Illinois Institute of Technology and Northwestern University (NUfab cleanroom)
